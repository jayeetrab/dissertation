# Methodology Fact Sheet
*Reference notes for writing Chapter 3. These are the verified facts of what
the system does — write your chapter FROM these, in your own words. Updated
7 July 2026.*

## Corpus
- 16 PDFs, 3,849 pages, 20,293 chunks after splitting and filtering
- 1 national document (NPPF December 2024, 82 pages, 243 numbered paragraphs)
- 11 English cities. Bristol = deep case study (5 documents, 695 pages,
  3,156 chunks); 10 breadth cities, one adopted plan each
- Per-city chunk counts: birmingham 1,014 · bristol 3,156 · leeds 1,403 ·
  leicester 1,823 · liverpool 1,837 · london 2,851 · national 556 ·
  newcastle 1,853 · nottingham 1,989 · plymouth 2,075 · sheffield 930 ·
  southampton 806
- Selection rationale: England only (NPPF scope — Scotland/Wales/NI have
  separate systems). Adopted development plans only, downloaded from the
  responsible LPA (full provenance: docs/corpus_sources.md)
- Plan adoption dates range 2009 (Sheffield) to June 2026 (Leicester) —
  a property of the plan-led system, enabling plan-age vs performance analysis

## Pipeline (backend/)
- Loading: PyPDFLoader, page-level, metadata: source_doc, page_number, city
  (city derived from docs/ subfolder — deterministic)
- Chunking: RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter, chunk_size=500 chars,
  overlap=100, min chunk length 50 chars
- Embeddings: OpenAI text-embedding-3-small (1,536 dims), stored in ChromaDB
- Spatial metadata: chunks tagged with city centroid coordinates
  (deterministic, from document provenance — no LLM). At QUERY time an LLM
  (gpt-4o-mini, structured output) extracts the location from the task text;
  chunks within 15 km get a similarity boost of up to +0.15 with over-fetch
  (4x TOP_K) then re-rank
- Retrieval: cosine similarity, TOP_K=8, cross-city by default (retrieving
  another city's policy is a measurable failure, not prevented); optional
  city filter exists for the filtered-retrieval ablation
- Generation: gpt-4o-mini, temperature 0.0 (reproducibility), max 1,500 tokens
- Baseline condition: same model and prompts, no retrieved context

## Task library
- 120 tasks: 40 Bristol (original deep set) + 80 multi-city (8 per city ×
  10 cities), across 5 categories: policy_compliance, evidence_synthesis,
  site_appraisal, stakeholder_communication, strategic_analysis
- Multi-city tasks are parameterised variants of the Bristol templates,
  generated by backend/generate_city_tasks.py (reproducible), citing each
  city's REAL policy codes — codes verified to exist in the plan text;
  two errors caught and corrected during verification (Leicester AH01
  didn't exist; Plymouth DEV8 covers Towns & Villages area, corrected to
  DEV7 for the Plymouth Policy Area) — then manually reviewed by the
  researcher [complete your review before writing this!]
- ST-02 per city is a deliberate misattribution probe (affordable housing
  thresholds differ per city)

## Evaluation design (three tiers)
1. **Automated, all outputs:**
   - RAGAS faithfulness + answer relevancy (gpt-4o-mini judge)
   - Retrieval city-mix per answer: % chunks from task city / national /
     other city (other-city = spatial misattribution signal)
   - Citation-existence check (backend/citation_check.py): every cited
     policy code checked against a ground-truth index of 1,052 policy codes
     + 243 NPPF paragraphs extracted from the corpus
     (backend/policy_index.py). Code exists nowhere → fabricated_clause
     (objective). Code exists only in another city's plan →
     spatial_misattribution (objective). LIMITATION: cannot detect
     outdated_policy or wrong content attributed to a real code — covered
     by RAGAS faithfulness and manual scoring
2. **Manual rubric** (accuracy 1-5, completeness 1-5, planning usefulness
   1-5, grounding 0-2, hallucination taxonomy): all ~80 Bristol outputs +
   stratified multi-city sample (2 tasks × 2 systems × 10 cities = 40
   outputs; fixed seed 42; backend/make_scoring_pack.py)
3. **Validation:** correlation between automated and manual scores on the
   ~120 dual-scored outputs justifies (or qualifies) automated-only scoring
   of the remainder

## Known limitations (be upfront — examiners reward honesty)
- One adopted plan per breadth city vs 5 documents for Bristol (corpus
  asymmetry)
- Newcastle and Plymouth plans are joint plans covering neighbouring areas
- London is a two-tier system: the London Plan 2021 is a strategic Spatial
  Development Strategy above 33 borough plans, so London is represented by its
  strategic plan rather than a borough DM plan (state as a scope choice). It
  replaced Manchester, whose Core Strategy was partially superseded by Places
  for Everyone (2024); the corpus now holds only current, unsuperseded plans
- gpt-4o-mini used both as system-under-test and RAGAS judge (same-family
  judge bias — cite the LLM-as-judge literature and the manual validation
  tier as mitigation)
- Automated citation check verifies existence, not correctness of content
